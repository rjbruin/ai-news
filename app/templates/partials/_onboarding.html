Welcome to Dispatch
What Dispatch does

Dispatch watches the news sources you point it at — newsletters, RSS/Atom feeds — pulls out the individual stories, and tags each one against a shared topic taxonomy. An AI agent then writes you an edition from whatever matches your interests, on whatever schedule you set.

Sources are shared, but configurable per person

Everyone using this instance shares the same pool of sources on the Sources page — no need to set up your own newsletter subscription if someone already has. Approved users can add their own sources too.

Don't want a particular source in your own editions? Turn it off just for yourself from the "In my editions" toggle on the Sources page — it stays on for everyone else.

Your edition is written for you

There's nothing to manually curate — the agent selects, groups, and writes up the stories that match your interests every time an edition is cut. Head to Settings to configure the release schedule, your interests, and how the content should be structured.

Once you have an edition, you can also export it as a printable PDF or generate a narrated podcast episode of it, if that's enabled for your account.

⚠️ This runs on AI models that cost money

Reading your sources and writing your editions uses AI models, and those cost real money per request. This app doesn't charge a subscription — instead, you bring your own OpenRouter API key to pay for the AI usage your account causes.

Before your own sources can be polled or your editions generated, set up a key on the API Keys page — it walks you through creating one, and gives you a rough idea of what it costs.
